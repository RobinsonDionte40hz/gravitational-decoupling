## The Mystery Signal - Key Observations

### 1. **Frequency: 38 mHz (26 second period)**
- Sharp, isolated spectral peak
- Detected using Horizontal-to-Vertical (H/V) spectral ratio
- Does not correspond to known seismic modes (not Rayleigh wave Airy phase at ~20 mHz)

### 2. **Timing: Appears "Several Minutes After" Surface Waves**
- **14:46** - Mainshock occurs
- **~14:50** - Surface waves pass through stations  
- **14:55-14:57** - **Peak 38 mHz signal detected**
- Time delay: **5-7 minutes after initial shaking**

### 3. **Duration: ~2 Minutes**
- Signal peaks around 14:55-14:57
- Then dissipates by 15:00
- Transient, not sustained

### 4. **Spatial Pattern: Non-Local**
- Detected at **all 382 GEONET stations** across northeastern Japan
- No geographical localization identified
- Regional phenomenon, not site-specific

### 5. **Authors' Conclusion:**
> "Since the origin of the 38mHz signal is unlikely to be local hydrologic tremors, tectonic tremors, or the tsunami, we speculate that the 38mHz signal originates from a kind of a characteristic oscillation of Northeastern Japan triggered by the 2011 Tohoku earthquake... **however, the detailed mechanism of the signal still remains a mystery.**"
